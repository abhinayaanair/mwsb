ABOUT US

The Malayalee Welfare Association - Bhiwadi (MWSB) stands as a beacon of hope, unity, and service for Malayalees residing in Rajasthan and neighboring states. Since its inception, MWSB has been deeply committed to preserving Kerala's rich cultural heritage while simultaneously addressing the real-life challenges faced by our community. Our mission is not just about celebration but about ensuring that every Malayalee living away from their homeland feels a sense of belonging, security, and pride.

Preserving Culture, Building Unity

MWSB plays a crucial role in keeping the spirit of Kerala alive among Malayalees living far from their homeland. We actively promote Kerala's traditions through cultural programs, Onam and Vishu celebrations, literary events, and Malayalam language workshops. These programs are not just about festivities, but about creating meaningful connections that allow individuals to relive the warmth and vibrancy of Kerala's culture. By engaging both the older and younger generations, we ensure that our rich heritage is carried forward with pride, passing on traditional knowledge and values to the next generation. Our organization serves as a cultural bridge, connecting Malayalees from different regions and backgrounds, and fostering unity among various Malayalee groups. Through these activities, we not only preserve our customs but also build a strong, cohesive community that continues to grow. We believe that by celebrating our cultural roots, we strengthen the bonds that tie us together and provide a sense of belonging, helping each individual to feel connected to Kerala no matter where they are.

MWSB's Efforts for a Greener and Healthier Bhiwadi

Recognizing the environmental challenges posed by rapid industrialization, MWSB has taken a proactive step towards combating pollution in Bhiwadi through large-scale tree plantation drives. With rising air pollution and declining green cover, we believe that sustainability starts with action. Our initiative not only helps purify the air but also nces the quality of life for the residents. By planting thousands of trees in key areas, we aim to create a natural shield against pollution, provide shade, and support biodiversity. Beyond just planting, we ensure the long-term care and tenance of these trees, fostering a culture of environmental responsibility. Through community involvement, awareness campaigns, and collaboration with local authorities, MWSB is committed to transforming Bhiwadi into a cleaner, healthier, and greener place to live.

A Legacy of Support and Service

When the world faced an unprecedented crisis during the COVID-19 pandemic, MWSB rose to the occasion. At a time when fear and uncertainty gripped everyone, and when very few were ready to take the initiative, we stepped forward to provide essential aid to those in need. Through our dedicated team of volunteers, we distributed ration kits to struggling families in Bhiwadi, ensuring that no one in our community went hungry. Our unwavering commitment to humanity was not just limited to providing food but also extended to helping people navigate medical emergencies, offering emotional support, and ensuring that no one felt abandoned in the face of adversity.

Beyond Crisis - A Commitment to Well-Being

MWSB doesn't limit itself to crisis management. Our work extends to holistic community development, with a focus on the overall well-being of every individual. We conduct free health check-up camps, providing access to quality healthcare for underprivileged sections of society, ensuring that everyone, regardless of their financial status, has the opportunity to receive medical attention. These initiatives are not only aimed at treating ailments but also at promoting awareness about healthy living and preventive care. From organizing blood donation drives to spreading awareness about preventive healthcare, we believe that a healthy community is a strong community. In addition to physical health, our programs also focus on mental well-being, offering counseling and support to those going through difficult times. Our efforts are rooted in the belief that a comprehensive approach to health and wellness will empower individuals to live fulfilling lives. These initiatives are designed to bridge the gap in medical access and ensure that no one in our community is deprived of the care they deserve, laying the foundation for a healthier, more resilient society.

Our Founders and Visionaries

MWSB was founded in October 2013 by a group of visionary individuals who believed in the strength of community and the importance of preserving our cultural heritage while creating a supportive environment for all Malayalees living away from home. The founding members, including Patron- Mr. R.V. Nair, President- Mr. K.R. Manoj, Vice President- Mr. Sivadas P Nair, Secretary- Mr. CK Babu, Joint Secretary- Mr. Sunil, and Treasurer- Mr. Philipose Daniel, were driven by a shared vision to build a strong, unified network that could cater to the needs of the Malayalee community in Rajasthan and beyond. Along with these key figures, 16 dedicated executive members—Mr. Unni Pillai, Mr. Ullas K S, Mrs. Vijayalakshmi KM, Mr. Sasi Nair, Mr. K.B. Nair, Mr. Johny Samuel, Mr. P.K. Kunji, Mr. Bose P.K., Mr. P.J. Prasad, Mr. Ashokan, Mr. Sivadas V Nair , Mr. S. Premachandran Nair, Mr. Issac Thomas, Mr. Chitranjan Nair, Mr. K.P. Nair, Mr. K Ramachandran, and Mr. Aji A P joined hands to lay the foundation of what would become a thriving community organization. Their collective efforts, hard work, and commitment to service have played an instrumental role in the growth of MWSB, ensuring that it remains a resilient and supportive community. Today, MWSB continues to flourish and serve the Malayalee diaspora, standing as a testament to the vision and determination of its founding members, who believed in the power of unity to overcome challenges and create lasting impact.

Looking Ahead

MWSB is committed to continuous growth, expanding our outreach, and strengthening our support system. Our vision extends beyond cultural preservation; we aim to nurture a vibrant, self-sufficient community where every Malayalee feels empowered, protected, and celebrated. With unwavering determination, we will continue to stand by our people, uphold the values of our heritage, and work towards a brighter future for Malayalees in Bhiwadi and beyond.
